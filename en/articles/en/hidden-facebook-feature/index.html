Hidden Facebook Feature: How “Weekly Missions” Might Help Small Businesses Get Seen Again

Many small business owners across Northern Europe continue using Facebook Pages to reach their communities — whether running a salon, a local café, or a design studio.
But in 2025, it’s clear: organic reach is harder to maintain. You may be sharing valuable content, but it often reaches only a fraction of your audience.

For those not ready to step away from Facebook yet, a lesser-known feature called Facebook Weekly Missions might be worth exploring.

What Are “Facebook Weekly Missions”?

Facebook (Meta) now assigns certain Page managers a set of weekly challenges designed to encourage consistent, engaging content. Each week, you may receive a few suggested actions in your Meta Business Suite.

Completing these tasks earns you a small badge — and more importantly, a temporary boost in visibility for your posts.

👉 This is not a generic list — every Page’s mission list is unique, depending on previous activity and account status.

Example Missions You May Receive

Here are some examples of common challenges Facebook might assign:

  • Post a Reel
    Share short-form video content to connect visually with your audience.

  • Respond to All Comments Within 24 Hours
    Keep conversations active and build trust by replying promptly.

  • Share an Image or a Link
    Add variety and value by posting strong visuals or relevant resources.

  • Schedule a Post in Advance
    Show consistency by planning your content ahead of time.

These missions change weekly. Completing them — no matter how small — contributes to better visibility and reach, especially when you’re managing social media alone.

Why This Matters

While it’s not a game-changing feature, Weekly Missions offer clear advantages:

  • 🧭 Direction – Ideal when you’re unsure what content to post
  • 🔄 Structure – Keeps your posting rhythm steady
  • 🔎 Visibility – Provides a mild but real boost in reach

For busy business owners, this feature delivers helpful reminders that align with how Facebook’s algorithm currently works.

Where to Find the Missions

To see if you have access, log into your Meta Business Suite, and check:

Page Tools → Missions or Tasks

📌 Note: This feature is being rolled out gradually. Some Pages will see it sooner than others.

Long-Term Perspective: Beyond the Algorithm

Features like this can help, but they’re still part of a closed system. The platform can change — or remove — anything at any time.
